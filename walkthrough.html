One-line summary

The AI Gateway is the governed control layer between a user's health context and AI systems. It decides what stays inside Consentext, what can be minimized, what can be sent out, and what should never happen off-board.

Primary job Classify, route, and audit every request.

What this website is

A governed AI product demo, not just a landing page

Who it is for

  • Founders explaining the product story to investors or partners.
  • Internal teammates learning the lane model and trust boundary.
  • Reviewers testing whether governance feels concrete and believable.

What problem it solves

  • Users should not have to choose between strong privacy and useful AI.
  • Health AI needs visible routing and exposure control, not hidden prompts.
  • Teams need a way to explain trust infrastructure as product behavior.

How to use the website

Recommended team workflow

1

Start with the live prototype

Open the main page and keep the default sample request or load a scenario from the prompt bank.

2

Run the gateway

Use the Run button to classify the request and populate lane, provider, exposure, packet, and response surfaces.

3

Read the output from top to bottom

Decision summary first, then selected lane, rationale, packet behavior, compare route, and audit receipt.

4

Use compare and scenarios for discussion

Compare mode is best for explaining why a route is chosen. Scenarios are best for repeatable demos and team reviews.

5

Capture feedback

Use founder review, lane sentiment, and session export when the goal is evaluation rather than presentation.

6

Use the source archive when challenged

Search the embedded source documents to show where the concepts, prompts, and design decisions came from.

Recommended live demo sequence

A six-step walkthrough for teammates and stakeholders

Step 1

Frame the product

Explain that this is a control layer for health AI, not another chatbot wrapper.

Step 2

Run a safe trust anchor request

Use a Deterministic or Private scenario first so the audience understands that not every request should go external.

Step 3

Show the minimization story

Use Private+ to compare richer vault context with the reduced export packet.

Step 4

Use compare mode

Show why the chosen lane is preferred over the nearest alternative and what control is preserved.

Step 5

Prove governance

Finish on execution trace, audit receipt, and the all-lanes snapshot view.

Step 6

Close with the strategic contrast

Use the Off-Board scenario to show what disappears when a user bypasses the gateway.

Highlights

The strongest parts of the current experience

What is working well

  • The lane model is visible and distinct.
  • The site clearly shows that routing happens before model interaction.
  • The audit trail makes governance feel like product behavior rather than a hidden policy layer.
  • The scenario pack makes the demo repeatable for different audiences.

What this makes easy to explain

  • Why not every request should be treated the same.
  • Why minimization matters for adoption.
  • Why off-board AI use weakens trust and control.
  • Why Consentext is positioned as infrastructure, not just an interface.

Current state

What this project is today

Current maturity

Interactive concept-review prototype

The site is a high-quality prototype that demonstrates routing logic, governance surfaces, demo scenarios, source grounding, and presentation support. It is not a production AI gateway or integrated health product.

What it is good for right now

  • Investor walkthroughs
  • Internal product alignment
  • Founder review and lane evaluation
  • Explaining trust design to partners or early collaborators
