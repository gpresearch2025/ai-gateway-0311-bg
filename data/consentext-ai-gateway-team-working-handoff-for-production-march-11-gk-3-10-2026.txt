Consentext AI Gateway – Team Working Handoff 
Purpose of This Document
This document is the current working explanation of the Consentext AI Gateway.
Its purpose is to help the internal team clearly understand what the AI Gateway is, what it is not, why it matters, how it relates to the Vault, and what should be explored in prototype form next.
This is not yet a final engineering architecture document.This is also not a marketing document.
This is a concept and decision document for the team.
The goal is to give the team a strong enough mental model that everyone can:
visualize the gateway clearly
understand how it fits into Consentext
see what is realistic to prototype now
identify what feels right and what still feels unresolved
make better decisions before the next round of technical scoping

Executive Summary
Consentext is not just trying to build another AI feature.
Consentext is trying to define a governed interaction layer between a user’s health context and AI systems.
That governed layer is what we are calling the AI Gateway.
The Gateway exists because the user should not have only two choices:
do not use AI at all
paste sensitive health information directly into an outside AI system
That binary is not acceptable for a health-focused product.
Instead, Consentext introduces a graduated model of AI access, where different kinds of AI interactions happen in different lanes, with different levels of privacy, control, and capability.
That is the role of the AI Gateway.

The High-Level Consentext System
The best way to understand the AI Gateway is to first understand that it is only one part of the broader Consentext system.
Consentext has at least three conceptual spaces:
USER  ↓CONSENTEXT SYSTEM  ├─ VAULT  ├─ HEALTH PORTAL  └─ AI GATEWAY
1. Vault
The Vault is the user-owned health data environment.
The Vault is broader than AI.
It may contain:
biomarkers
lab history
wearable device metrics
nutrition data
fitness information
uploaded records
medical documents
longitudinal health patterns
future structured and unstructured health context
The Vault is the protected home of the user’s health context.
2. Health Portal
The Health Portal is the system that enables users to share information from the Vault with:
doctors
clinics
hospitals
healthcare systems
This pathway is separate from AI usage.
The Health Portal is about clinical sharing and coordination.
3. AI Gateway
The AI Gateway is the system that governs how AI is allowed to interact with the user’s health context.
This is not the same thing as the Vault.
This is not the same thing as the Portal.
This is the system that answers the question:
How can the user benefit from AI without losing control of their health data?

Why the AI Gateway Exists
The AI Gateway exists because health data is too sensitive for a simple “AI on / AI off” model.
Most consumer AI usage today looks like this:
User → AI
Or slightly better:
User → App → AI
But Consentext is trying to create something more responsible:
User ↓Vault ↓Gateway ↓AI
That middle layer is the point.
The Gateway is the system that decides:
what kind of request this is
how sensitive the request is
whether AI is needed at all
whether the AI interaction should remain fully inside Consentext
whether a reduced or de-identified packet can leave
whether broader external AI usage is allowed
what must be logged and auditable

What the AI Gateway Is
The AI Gateway is best understood as a governance layer and a control system.
It is the part of Consentext that governs how health context is used with AI.
In plain language:
The AI Gateway decides:
which lane a request belongs in
what information is allowed to be used
whether anything is allowed to leave Consentext
what kind of AI interaction is appropriate
how the interaction is recorded
A simple one-line definition:
The AI Gateway determines how a user request interacts with AI and what health context is allowed to be used or shared.

What the AI Gateway Is Not
This distinction is very important.
At least right now, the AI Gateway is not:
a prompt optimization engine
a giant multi-model orchestration brain
a system primarily focused on making AI as smart as possible
a replacement for the model labs
just a chat assistant
just an API layer
just a model router
just a privacy policy wrapper
Consentext is not trying to replace OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, Google, Alibaba, or other model providers.
Consentext is trying to become the trusted consumer-controlled layer that helps people use those systems responsibly with health context.

Feature vs Control System
One of the most important distinctions we uncovered in this session is the difference between a feature and a control system.
A feature answers:
What can the user do?
Examples:
explain my lab results
summarize my health records
compare treatments
suggest lifestyle changes
A control system answers:
How is the system allowed to operate?
That is the AI Gateway.
The Gateway is not “the feature.”
The Gateway governs how all AI-related features are allowed to behave.
Another way to say it:
A feature performs a task.A control system governs how tasks are allowed to be performed.
That is why the Gateway matters so much.

The Best Mental Model
The most useful mental model so far is:
The Vault stores.
The Gateway governs.
The AI reasons.
That is a very helpful separation.

The AI Exposure Spectrum
The Gateway introduces multiple levels of AI exposure.
Instead of one kind of AI interaction, there are several.
MOST PRIVATE                                    MOST AI POWER|----------------------------------------------------------------|Deterministic → Private → Private+ → Max Intelligence
And separately:
OFF THE BOARD = User goes directly to outside AI without Consentext
Each lane represents a different balance of:
privacy
capability
control

The AI Gateway Triangle
Another helpful visual model is the triangle between:
                PRIVACY                   /\                  /  \                 /    \                /      \               /        \        CONTROL --------- CAPABILITY
Privacy
How much sensitive health context remains inside Consentext.
Capability
How much AI reasoning and power the user can access.
Control
How strongly Consentext governs routing, redaction, boundaries, permissions, and approvals.
The lanes exist because no single point on this triangle is always correct for every user request.

The Four Lanes

Lane 1 – Deterministic
Definition
No AI is used.
This lane uses structured logic, calculations, timelines, thresholds, extraction rules, and templated outputs.
Example requests
Show my cholesterol trend
List my recent lab dates
Show my biomarker changes over six months
Identify missing lab panels
Show medication timeline
Produce structured daily score summary
What this lane means
The system is not reasoning like an LLM.It is calculating, retrieving, and formatting.
What leaves Consentext
Nothing.
Strategic role
This is the trust anchor.
It proves the system can create value without external AI exposure.
Why it matters
This lane gives users confidence that not every health question requires AI.
It also creates a foundation for later trust in the more advanced lanes.

Lane 2 – Private
Definition
AI is used, but the sensitive health record data stays inside Consentext-controlled systems.
Example requests
Explain my lab results
Help me understand this pattern in my wearable data
Summarize my recent biomarkers in plain English
Explain this medical record in simpler language
What this lane means
AI can help, but it does so inside the protected environment.
This is the “AI inside the wall” concept.
Internal model example
For prototype purposes, Qwen can be used as the example internal model.
The point is not that Qwen defines the gateway.The point is that an internal model can help demonstrate what a protected-AI lane looks like.
What leaves Consentext
Sensitive data should not leave.
Strategic role
This is the highest-privacy AI lane.
Why it matters
This lane is essential because it gives the user AI help without forcing them into immediate external data exposure.

Lane 3 – Private+
Definition
External AI may be used, but the gateway sends only reduced, minimal, or de-identified context.
Example requests
What lifestyle changes might improve these labs?
What general interventions are associated with this pattern?
What habits may affect these metrics?
Example reduced context packet
Instead of sending the full record:
Age: 54Sex: MaleLDL: 168HDL: 40Triglycerides: 210
The gateway should send only the minimum necessary context needed for the task.
What this lane means
The raw vault stays protected.A smaller, safer packet may travel.
What leaves Consentext
Only approved reduced context.
Strategic role
This is the adoption bridge.
It gives the user meaningful AI support while still applying privacy guardrails.
Why it matters
This lane may become one of the most practically useful lanes in the entire system.

Lane 4 – Max Intelligence
Definition
This lane allows the broadest external AI interaction.
Example requests
Compare treatment approaches
Analyze research directions
Compare competing health hypotheses
Review general medical literature around these patterns
What this lane means
The user wants the strongest AI capability available.
More context may be shared depending on user choice and system rules.
What leaves Consentext
Potentially broader context, subject to the gateway’s rules.
Strategic role
This preserves user freedom and keeps Consentext in front of the interaction.
Why it matters
Without this lane, Consentext may become too restrictive for users who want powerful external AI capability.

Off-Board Interaction
This is when the user bypasses Consentext and goes directly to a model lab UI.
Examples:
ChatGPT
Claude
Gemini
other external AI chat tools
What this means
The user is no longer using Consentext’s protections.
What Consentext loses
routing control
minimization
redaction
audit trail
consent governance
interaction boundary
Why it matters
This needs to be understood clearly because it may become one of the strongest arguments for why Consentext should exist at all.

The Gateway Decision Flow
The simplest conceptual flow is:
User submits request        ↓Gateway identifies the task type        ↓Gateway applies trust / exposure rules        ↓Gateway selects the lane        ↓Lane executes the task        ↓Response returned        ↓Audit record created
This is the current best simplification of the gateway’s role.

Audit and Interaction Receipts
Every gateway-mediated AI interaction should conceptually generate an audit record.
Possible fields:
timestamp
user request
lane used
data accessed
data exported
AI provider used
consent scope
notes on redaction or minimization
This matters because the gateway is not just about routing.It is also about traceability and proof of controlled use.

Why the Gateway May Matter More Over Time
Even if we do not force the narrative now, there is a reason the idea of the gateway becoming “the brain of the system” feels tempting.
If the gateway eventually learns to:
classify intent
determine safe exposure levels
manage human + agent + model interactions
control context release
mediate permissions
maintain audit boundaries
then it may evolve from being a policy router into something closer to a context engine.
We should not force that conclusion yet.
But it is a very reasonable future direction to keep in mind.

Agents and the Future Boundary Problem
One of the important forward-looking ideas discussed in this session is that future AI systems will increasingly include agents.
If agents become part of the product or broader ecosystem, they must not access the Vault directly.
The safer future pattern looks more like:
Agent → Gateway → Vault
Not:
Agent → Vault
This is one reason the gateway may become even more important over time.
It is not just a human-to-AI routing layer.
It may eventually become the permission boundary between:
humans
agents
AI models
health data

Qwen and the Internal Model Discussion
A team member raised Qwen as a possibly useful model for helping prove the concept.
That is a reasonable idea.
Where Qwen fits
Qwen can be a strong prototype example for the Private lane.
Why
it helps demonstrate local or internal inference
it supports the “AI inside the wall” idea
it can reduce prototype dependence on outside commercial APIs
it satisfies the conceptual need for a protected-AI lane
Important note
Qwen does not define the gateway architecture.
It is just a useful model example that may sit inside one lane.
The gateway design remains the same regardless of whether the internal model is Qwen, another open model, or a future internal setup.

What the Team Should Decide Tonight
The team should not try to answer every future architecture question tonight.
Instead, the team should focus on a smaller set of concept questions.
1. Do the lanes make intuitive sense?
Deterministic
Private
Private+
Max Intelligence
Off-board
2. Does the gateway feel like the correct control point?
User → Vault → Gateway → AI
3. Does this solve a meaningful real-world problem?
Specifically:
How can people use powerful AI with sensitive health data without losing control?
4. Does the team agree that the gateway is a control system, not just a feature?
5. Does the Private lane feel important enough to prototype with a model like Qwen?

What the Team Should Not Overcomplicate Yet
For now, the team should not get lost in:
final model vendor strategy
prompt engineering
advanced agentic design
final architecture specs
production-grade compliance details
final API routing stack
full inference infrastructure
Those can come later.
Right now the task is to make the gateway concept real enough to understand, test, and refine.

Working Definition to Carry Forward
Here is the best current working definition:
Consentext AI Gateway is the governed interaction layer that determines how a user’s health context can be used with AI, routes each request through the appropriate privacy-capability lane, and preserves control through minimization, boundaries, and auditability.

Closing Thought
The Vault is broader than AI.The Health Portal is broader than AI.The AI Gateway is specifically about governed AI interaction.
If Consentext gets this right, it could become a uniquely valuable control layer in a world where AI grows more powerful, more agentic, and more embedded in personal decision-making.
For now, the next step is not to overstate it.
The next step is to understand it clearly enough to prototype it honestly.
